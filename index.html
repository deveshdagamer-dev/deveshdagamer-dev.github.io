Experimental materials chemistry

Dr. Devesh Chandra Binwal

Experimental Materials Chemist | Halide Perovskites | Crystal Growth | Magnetism | TMDs/HER

I am a Research Associate at Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research (JNCASR), Bengaluru, India. I completed my M.S. Engineering in 2021 under the supervision of Prof. C. N. R. Rao, FRS, and my PhD at JNCASR in 2025 under the supervision of Prof. C. N. R. Rao, FRS and Dr. Pratap Vishnoi.

My research asks why crystalline materials behave the way they do, and how structure can be used to tune optical, magnetic, and catalytic properties. I work on halide perovskites, composition and dimensionality control including Li-doped perovskite directions, single-crystal growth and SCXRD, magnetic materials, layered materials and TMDs, and MoS2-based systems for the hydrogen evolution reaction (HER), relevant to hydrogen production.

Materials chemistry, crystallography, and magnetic halide perovskites.

Research focus

Structure-driven design of functional materials

My work connects synthesis, single-crystal structures, and physical properties so that composition and dimensionality become useful design variables rather than only descriptive features.

Magnetic halide perovskites

Mo(III)-based halide double perovskites used as clean S = 3/2 platforms for studying exchange pathways and dimensionality effects.

Single-crystal growth and SCXRD

Solution growth, structure solution, and refinement of low-dimensional halide materials with attention to composition, symmetry, and bond geometry.

Magnetism and phase transitions

Low-temperature magnetic ordering, reversible phase transitions, antiferromagnetic exchange, and structure-property correlations in open-shell inorganic frameworks.

TMDs and hydrogen evolution

MoS2, MoSe2, WS2, Mo2C, and related nanocomposites for photocatalytic and electrocatalytic HER.
